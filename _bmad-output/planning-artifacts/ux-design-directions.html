Design Direction Comparison

⭐ SELECTED

7. Card Stack + Floating Pill

PROS

  • Best of both worlds: focused scoring + standings awareness
  • Floating pill is non-intrusive yet always accessible
  • Hole-by-hole navigation keeps mental model clear
  • Expandable sheet provides full leaderboard detail on demand
  • Position changes add competitive context
  • Swipe navigation feels natural and fluid

CONS

  • Slightly more complex than single-view approaches
  • Pill may feel redundant to some users
  • Requires implementation of overlay/sheet patterns
  • More UI states to design and test

1. Leaderboard First

PROS

  • Competition visibility is paramount
  • Simple mental model: one screen
  • Inline expansion minimizes navigation
  • Quick glance shows everyone's status

CONS

  • Scoring feels secondary to standings
  • Expansion pattern might feel cluttered
  • No hole context visible at a glance
  • Requires tap to enter scores

2. Split Stage

PROS

  • Zero context switching required
  • Always aware of standings + current hole
  • Compact leaderboard feels efficient
  • Scoring area gets dedicated space

CONS

  • Leaderboard is visually compressed
  • May feel cramped on smaller screens
  • Less dramatic/focused than alternatives
  • Fixed split may not suit all preferences

3. Card Stack

PROS

  • Each hole feels like a distinct moment
  • Swipe navigation is intuitive/familiar
  • Mini bar keeps standings accessible
  • Easy to review previous/next holes

CONS

  • Horizontal swiping adds friction
  • Mini bar is very compressed
  • Pull-down overlay adds complexity
  • Navigation requires explicit action

4. Dashboard

PROS

  • Maximum information density
  • Everything visible at once
  • Activity feed adds social context
  • Appeals to power users

CONS

  • Can feel overwhelming/cluttered
  • Smaller touch targets in grid
  • Not ideal for casual/beginner users
  • Reduced visual hierarchy

5. Focus Mode

PROS

  • Absolute minimal cognitive load
  • Perfect for beginners/casual play
  • Large, easy-to-hit controls
  • Clear single-task focus

CONS

  • Slower for entering multiple scores
  • Requires cycling through players
  • No competitive context while scoring
  • May feel too simplified for experienced users

6. Live Feed

PROS

  • Emphasizes group/social dynamics
  • Natural for distributed scoring
  • Activity history is transparent
  • Floating pill is non-intrusive

CONS

  • Feed can feel noisy/overwhelming
  • Less clear "where to enter score"
  • Pill doesn't show full standings
  • Novelty factor may wear off
